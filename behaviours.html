Online Behaviours

Online behaviours are the way you act or treat people online or by communications such as a mobile phone.

Acceptable behaviour online and at school

- Using computers correctly and for work-related purpsoes

- Listening to everyone's opinions and being respectful

- Being supportive and helping a friend if they are being bullied

- Reporting harmful or unsafe online behaviours to a person of authority


Unacceptable behaviour online and at school

- Taking photos or videos of students or teachers without their consent

- Purposefully creating trouble online such as spreading rumours or being disrespectful

- Using inappropriate, discriminitory or offensive language online

- Making cruel or offensive comments to or about other people


How Viewbank College promptes Cybersafety

Student Computer and Internet Use

- Students are required to protect their privacy and personal details

- Students are required to protect the privacy of others by not posting their personal details without their consent

- Students must not use ICT to harass, threaten, discriminate, intimidate or defame others

- Students must use the internet for a valid purpose relating to school related purposes

- Students must uphold copyright regulation by not plagarising the work of others
